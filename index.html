üëÄ The Eyes (Sonar)

Seeing with Sound

Our robot doesn't use cameras. It uses Sound Waves! This is called "Ultrasonic."

It works just like a bat in a cave:

  • 1. The robot shouts a "beep" (we can't hear it).
  • 2. The beep hits your leg.
  • 3. The beep bounces back to the robot.

üìè The Math

Measuring Distance

How does the robot know if you are close or far?

It counts the time!

If the beep comes back fast: You are CLOSE.

If the beep takes a long time: You are FAR AWAY.

Maier, Ayan, and Abdul Rehman have to use math to teach the robot how to count very, very fast (in microseconds!).

üö¶ The 3 Rules

The Robot's Instructions

We are going to give the robot three simple rules to follow:

  • Rule 1 (The STOP Rule): If the person is too close (less than 15cm), STOP immediately so we don't crash!
  • Rule 2 (The GO Rule): If the person is walking away (20cm to 50cm), drive forward to catch up.
  • Rule 3 (The WAIT Rule): If the person is gone, stop and wait for them to come back.

üí™ The Muscles (Motors)

Making it Move

The Arduino brain is smart, but it is not strong. It cannot push the robot.

So, we use DC Motors. These are the muscles. They spin the wheels. We use a special part called a "Motor Driver" to give the muscles lots of power from the battery.
