Project Initiation: Team BETA

Autonomous Follow-Me Robot Strategy

Team BETA, composed of Maier, Ayan, and Abdul Rehman, is entering the primary planning phase for an Autonomous Follow-Me Robotic platform. This project is designed to bridge the gap between theoretical electronics and physical automation. Our initial research focuses on creating a reliable tracking system using ultrasonic wave propagation.

The objective of this phase is to define the hardware requirements and develop a logical roadmap for the system's architecture. By establishing clear engineering goals now, we ensure that our future build will be stable, responsive, and efficient. This presentation outlines our blueprint for the upcoming development cycle.

PHASE: PLANNING_01/20

The Central Logic Unit

Selecting the Arduino Platform

For our central processor, the team has selected the Arduino platform. Our planning dictates that we need a controller with sufficient I/O pins and a clock speed capable of processing sonar data without significant latency. The Arduino is ideal because it allows for rapid prototyping and has a robust library system for motor control.

During the planning phase, Maier, Ayan, and Abdul Rehman are mapping out the specific digital and analog pins required to interface with the sensors and drivers. This documentation is critical to ensure that our future software code aligns perfectly with the physical hardware connections.

PHASE: PLANNING_02/20

Proposed Wiring Schematic

Design and Signal Path Planning

A critical part of our current planning is the Technical Wiring Map. We are designing a layout that prioritizes signal integrity. This involves planning the paths for the 5V logic lines and the 9V power lines. Team BETA is focused on a "Star Ground" configuration to minimize electrical noise that could interfere with our distance readings.

The schematic we are developing will serve as the master guide during the assembly phase. By documenting every jumper wire and resistor value now, Maier, Ayan, and Abdul Rehman are taking a professional "Design First" approach to robotics, significantly reducing the risk of short circuits or logic errors later.

PHASE: PLANNING_03/20

Spatial Perception Planning

HC-SR04 Sensor Integration

To give the robot "vision," we have planned the integration of the HC-SR04 Ultrasonic Sensor. Our research indicates this is the most effective low-cost solution for indoor proximity detection. Our planning includes determining the optimal mounting height on the chassis to ensure a clear line of sight for the 40kHz acoustic pulses.

The sensor will be the primary source of input data for our autonomous logic. During this planning stage, we are calculating the necessary delay times between "pings" to avoid "ghost echoes"—a phenomenon where old sound waves interfere with new distance readings.

PHASE: PLANNING_04/20

Theoretical Calculations

Sonar Distance Modeling

Our planning involves establishing the mathematical foundations of the robot. We are using the speed of sound—343 meters per second—as our constant. Team BETA is planning the implementation of the formula: Distance = (Time × 0.034) / 2.

By modeling these calculations before writing the code, Maier, Ayan, and Abdul Rehman can predict the robot's accuracy. We are also planning to implement a "Running Average" algorithm to smooth out sensor data, which will prevent the robot from reacting to small fluctuations in the ultrasonic readings.

PHASE: PLANNING_05/20

Algorithm Architecture

Autonomous Decision Tree

The team is currently designing the Decision Logic Flowchart. This will be the "Mind" of the robot. We are planning a state-machine architecture that constantly evaluates the following conditions:

  • Proximity Brake: If the target is within a 15cm safety threshold, stop all motor activity.
  • Tracking Mode: If the target is between 20cm and 50cm, maintain forward velocity.
  • Search State: If the target is lost (Distance > 100cm), enter an idle scanning mode.

This logical planning ensures that the robot behaves predictably and safely in all scenarios.

PHASE: PLANNING_06/20

Motor Driver Specifications

L298N H-Bridge Strategy

The L298N Motor Driver is our planned interface for mechanical movement. Because the Arduino cannot drive motors directly, this module is a requirement. Our planning involves configuring the H-Bridge pins to allow for bi-directional control, which is essential for steering.

Maier, Ayan, and Abdul Rehman are studying the thermal properties of the L298N to ensure it does not overheat during extended use. We are also planning the jumper settings for the "Enable" pins to allow for Pulse Width Modulation (PWM) speed control.

PHASE: PLANNING_07/20

Mechanical Drivetrain

DC Motor and Torque Requirements

We are planning to use two geared DC motors for the robot’s propulsion. Our focus is on Torque over speed. Geared motors provide the necessary force to move the chassis while allowing for precise, low-speed maneuvers.

The team is also evaluating wheel diameters to determine the "RPM to Velocity" ratio. This planning is vital because it determines how fast the robot can catch up to a user who is walking at a normal pace.

PHASE: PLANNING_08/20

Interface Pinout Planning

Arduino Configuration Map

An essential step in our planning is the Pinout Table. We are assigning specific digital pins for the Trigger and Echo functions of the HC-SR04, and PWM-capable pins for the motor driver speed control.

This planning avoids "Pin Conflicts"—a common mistake where two components try to use the same internal timer of the Arduino. Maier, Ayan, and Abdul Rehman have documented these pins to ensure the future coding phase proceeds without hardware-related bugs.

PHASE: PLANNING_09/20

Energy Source Strategy

Battery and Voltage Regulation

Team BETA is planning a 9V power delivery system. Our primary concern during this planning stage is Voltage Stability. We are designing a power rail that separates the "noisy" motor current from the "clean" logic current.

We are also planning to include an external switch for safety and a voltage monitoring system. This allows the team to ensure the batteries have enough charge to power both the motors and the processing logic simultaneously.

PHASE: PLANNING_10/20

Prototype Simulation

Virtual Testing Phase

Part of our current planning is to simulate the circuit using CAD software before any wires are cut. This allows Maier, Ayan, and Abdul Rehman to visualize the flow of electricity. We are using these simulations to identify potential bottlenecks in the data flow. By virtually testing the sonar's field of view, we can determine exactly where to place it on the chassis for maximum tracking efficiency.

PHASE: PLANNING_11/20

Environmental Signal Analysis

Accounting for Interference

Our planning includes an analysis of environmental interference. Ultrasonic waves can be absorbed by carpets or reflected poorly by glass. Team BETA is planning to write a "Calibration Routine" into the software. This will allow the robot to adjust its sensitivity based on the room it is in. This advanced planning ensures the robot will work in a classroom, a lab, or a hallway with equal reliability.

PHASE: PLANNING_12/20

Chassis Structural Design

Material Selection and Balance

The team is evaluating acrylic vs. 3D-printed materials for the chassis frame. Our planning focuses on Structural Integrity. We need a base that is rigid enough to hold the motors in place but light enough to not drain the batteries. We are planning to place the battery pack at the very center of the chassis to ensure a balanced center of gravity, preventing the robot from tipping during fast stops.

PHASE: PLANNING_13/20

Software Development Roadmap

Iterative Coding Strategy

The coding phase is planned to be iterative. We will start with a simple "Stop/Go" script and then move to "Proportional Speed Control." Proportional control means the robot will slow down smoothly as it gets closer to the user, rather than stopping abruptly. This planning is what will make the Team BETA robot look professional and sophisticated compared to basic hobby projects.

PHASE: PLANNING_14/20

PWM Tuning and Frequency

Motor Speed Optimization

A key planning objective is to determine the ideal PWM frequency for our DC motors. If the frequency is too low, the motors will make a buzzing sound; if it's too high, they lose torque. Maier and the team are planning to test different duty cycles to find the "Sweet Spot" where the motors are quiet, strong, and responsive. This level of detail is a core part of our engineering planning.

PHASE: PLANNING_15/20

Risk Assessment & Mitigation

Ensuring System Reliability

No engineering project is without risk. Team BETA is currently planning for "Worst Case Scenarios," such as a sensor failing or a motor stalling. We are planning to include "Failsafe Code" that will cut power to the motors if the Arduino stops receiving valid data from the sonar sensor. This proactive planning is essential for the safety of the hardware and the users.

PHASE: PLANNING_16/20

Scaling to Industry Standards

Real-World Application Study

As part of our planning, we are studying how industrial giants like Amazon and Tesla handle autonomous tracking. While we are using an Arduino, the logic of "Distance Maintenance" we are planning is a simplified version of the algorithms used in self-driving cars. This project is a foundational step in our journey to becoming professional roboticists.

PHASE: PLANNING_17/20

Team Communication Protocols

Managing a Multi-Disciplinary Project

Team BETA recognizes that communication is as important as the circuit. We are planning weekly "Design Reviews" where Maier, Ayan, and Abdul Rehman will check each other’s work. This collaborative planning ensures that the mechanical build fits the electrical wiring, and the electrical wiring supports the software requirements. Total team synergy is our goal.

PHASE: PLANNING_18/20

Projected Timeline

Milestones and Deadlines

Our planning includes a strict timeline. Week 1 is for circuit simulation. Week 2 is for chassis assembly. Week 3 is for core logic programming. By breaking the project into these planned segments, Maier, Ayan, and Abdul Rehman can ensure that the project is submitted on time without rushing the critical testing phases.

PHASE: PLANNING_19/20

Conclusion of Planning Phase

Readiness for Construction

In conclusion, Team BETA has developed a comprehensive and professional plan for the Follow-Me Robot. We have defined our hardware, mapped our software logic, and analyzed the physics of our sensors. We are now ready to move from the planning phase into the physical construction phase with total confidence.
